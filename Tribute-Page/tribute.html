Dr.Magdi Yacoub

The man who saved Children's lives

Store of Dr.Magdi Yacoub

  • Sir Magdi Habib Yacoub (born November 16, 1935) is an Egyptian-British Professor and a prominent cardiac surgeon.
  • Born in Belbeis, Sharkia Governorate, Egypt, to a Coptic Orthodox family
  • He studied medicine at Cairo University, learned in Chicago, then moved to Britain in 1962 to work at the Chest Hospital in London
  • In 1980, he performed a heart transplant for Drake Morris.
  • Among the celebrities he has operated on is British comedian Eric Morecambe
  • In 1966, he is called the "King of Hearts" in the British media.
  • In 2001, when he was 65 years old, he retired from surgery and continued as a consultant and theorist for organ transplantation
  • In 1983, he performed a heart transplant for an Englishman named John McCafferty. Because of that surgery, he entered the Guinness Book of Records as the tallest person to live with a heart transplant, for a period of 33 years until John passed away in 2016.
